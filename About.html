More About Me

Hi, I'm Japnam! I'm a second-year Computing Science student at Simon Fraser University and a developer passionate about solving problems and building things that matter. My journey into tech started with a high school programming course that sparked my interest in how technology shapes our lives—and how I could be part of creating it. Since then, I've grown my skills through courses in software development, data structures, algorithms, and computer systems, while also improving how I collaborate, think critically, and learn fast under pressure.

I enjoy turning ideas into real, functional solutions—especially when I get to work with others and find creative ways to approach a challenge. Whether I'm coding solo or in a team, I value clean, thoughtful design and a constant willingness to improve.

Outside of tech, I'm a huge hockey fan and a loyal (and very hopeful) Canucks supporter. When I'm not watching the game, I'm probably outside biking, hiking, or working on motorcycles. I love understanding how things work—whether it's through code or a machine—and I'm always up for learning something new or discovering a new trail, café, or side project. For me, growth is about staying curious and staying connected, both in tech and in life.
